The Scarcity of Obesity

Have you ever been unhappy about yourself when you looked in the mirror? Obesity could be one of the reasons why you think this way. Obesity is a growing global health challenge that affects millions of people and poses serious risks to long-term well-being. Defined by excessive body fat accumulation, it is often measured using the Body Mass Index (BMI), a simple calculation based on weight and height that helps categorize individuals into healthy, overweight, or obese ranges.

However, obesity is more than just a number — it is shaped by a complex mix of lifestyle choices, dietary habits, physical activity levels, and more. This page explores these influences, offering a comprehensive look at how such a scarce issue has developed in Australia and worldwide, its health impacts and correlations, and the steps individuals and societies can take to prevent and manage it as a way forward.
